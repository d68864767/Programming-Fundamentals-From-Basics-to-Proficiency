Title: "Programming Fundamentals: From Basics to Proficiency"

#### References

This eBook has been created with the help of numerous resources. Here are some of the key references that were used in the creation of this book:

1. "Introduction to Programming in Java: An Interdisciplinary Approach" by Robert Sedgewick and Kevin Wayne
2. "Python Crash Course: A Hands-On, Project-Based Introduction to Programming" by Eric Matthes
3. "Eloquent JavaScript: A Modern Introduction to Programming" by Marijn Haverbeke
4. "You Don't Know JS: Up & Going" by Kyle Simpson
5. "The Pragmatic Programmer: Your Journey to Mastery" by Andrew Hunt and David Thomas
6. "Clean Code: A Handbook of Agile Software Craftsmanship" by Robert C. Martin
7. "Code Complete: A Practical Handbook of Software Construction" by Steve McConnell
8. "Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software" by Erich Gamma, Richard Helm, Ralph Johnson, and John Vlissides
9. "Refactoring: Improving the Design of Existing Code" by Martin Fowler
10. "Introduction to the Theory of Computation" by Michael Sipser

In addition to these books, various online resources such as Stack Overflow, Mozilla Developer Network (MDN), and W3Schools were also used extensively.

Please note that while we have done our best to provide accurate and up-to-date information, programming is a rapidly evolving field, and it is always a good idea to refer to the official documentation of the respective programming languages and tools for the most accurate information.
